Problem Solving

14 December 2019

"A time I had got blocked on a simple problem?"

During Sprint 4 I had spent alot of time stuck on question 6 of the Manipulating JS Objects Challenge to the point where I had decided to comment out the question in order to complete the rest of the challenge. Much to my own dismay it turns out that I had completely misread the question and had tried coming up with my own way of solving the solution not realising that all I had to do was set Terah's spouses spouse equal to Terah.

"A time I had solved a problem in an elegant way?"

During the same challenge (after I had commented out question 6) I felt that everything was coming together and I was able to get to a solution without having to Google or ask my peers/coaches for help.

"How confident I felt using the problem solving techniques and process?"

  • Pseudocode

    • I'm confident writing Pseudocode since it gives me a rough idea of where and how I could start writing my code.

  • Trying Something

    • I feel confident using this technique and tend to do this alot before I ask my peers or coaches for help.

  • Rubber Ducky Method

    • I haven't yet tried this method but I think I would be confident using it but only as a last resort.

  • Reading Error Messages

    • Confident with this. Often I'll read through error messages first before doing anything else.

  • Console Logging

    • I'm confident using the console but to be honest I don't do this enough.

  • Googling

    • I'm really confident turning to Google for help. Pretty much 85-90% of my problem solving strategy revolves around Googling for an answer(s).

  • Asking your peers for help

    • I'm not that confident using this technique in fact I tend to avoid doing it unless it's absolutely necessary.

  • Asking coaches for help

    • Similar to asking peers for help I tend to also avoid asking coaches for help unless I absolutely have to.

  • Improving your process with reflection

    • I haven't used this technique yet however I would be confident using it at the end of every difficult challenge.
